Today we’ll spend primarily working through assignment 1. A few notes before we get started:

Something for everyone

As we said at the start of the course, our goal is to start off by tossing you in the deep end. We’ve got some pretty experienced folk, so we’ve had to toss pretty hard – hopefully everyone is kicking water now. Keep in mind that I am looking for somewhat different things depending on where we are all starting, which gives you and your partner a couple different possible strategies.

Are you and your partner on the same page? This is supposed to be a collaboration, not an internship. If you feel your partner is way ahead of you or way behind you, let me know. Everyone will learn more in this course with a roughly equal partner. Of course you still can (and are expected to) get help from more advanced pairs and help out other pairs, but this is not a spectator sport.

In deep water?

Yay, data science usually feels like that for all of us. So if this is all feeling really new: don’t worry about getting everything done and everything perfect. You have two possible stategies:

  • Breath first try and work through as much as you can of each of the exercises, skipping anything you get stuck on (rolling averages, fancy plots, better formatted code). This will get you the most practice reading in and maybe plotting different data; there will be time to master the data transformation dplyr stuff later, as well as making pretty output reports on GitHub.

  • Depth first pick one or a few questions to get very polished. Pay attention to code style, ask about formatting tricks to make your output presentable, get solid on GitHub-RMarkdown thing.

Either way, ask questions and see what others are doing.

Old hand?

If for some reason this is all seems too easy, then the fun is just beginning. Aim for polished code in a polished markdown output document – is your code easy to read and consistent with the style guide? Can you perform all steps of the analysis using the packages and patterns stated in the tidyverse manifesto? Think of your assignment as a report and think about your audience of other researchers. How does your report compare to the NASA website presenting this information?

Your other task as an experienced data scientist is in bringing your colleagues along. Aim to teach with questions and not by typing answers.
